Nexus Agile Framework Tutorial

Introduction

Nexus is an Agile framework designed to scale Scrum for large product and software development initiatives.
It addresses the challenges that arise when multiple Scrum teams work together on a single product, such as coordination, integration, and communication.
By using Nexus, organizations can ensure that all teams are aligned, working efficiently, and delivering high-quality integrated increments.

Why Use the Nexus Agile Framework?

  • Enhanced Coordination: Nexus facilitates better coordination among multiple Scrum teams, reducing duplication of efforts and miscommunication.
  • Improved Integration: The framework ensures that the work of all teams is seamlessly integrated into a single, cohesive product increment.
  • Consistent Delivery: Nexus promotes continuous and consistent delivery of high-quality products by maintaining a clear focus on integration and alignment.

What is Nexus?

Nexus is a framework that builds upon Scrum, providing additional structure for scaling Scrum to handle large-scale, complex projects involving multiple Scrum teams.
It introduces specific roles, events, and artifacts to manage dependencies and integration challenges effectively.

Nexus Integration Team

A team responsible for ensuring that the work of all Scrum teams is integrated and meets the Definition of Done.

Nexus Events

Including Nexus Sprint Planning, Nexus Daily Scrum, Nexus Sprint Review, and Nexus Sprint Retrospective, which facilitate coordination and integration across teams.

Nexus Artifacts

Such as the Nexus Sprint Backlog, Integrated Increment, and Nexus Goal, which help manage and track the work of multiple teams.

How Does It Work?

Setting Up Nexus

  1. Forming Teams: Create multiple Scrum teams and a Nexus Integration Team.
  2. Defining Roles: Clearly define the roles and responsibilities of each team member.
  3. Setting Goals: Establish a Nexus Goal to align all teams.

Running Nexus Events

  • Nexus Sprint Planning: Teams collaboratively plan their sprints, focusing on integration and coordination.

    Example: Each team identifies their sprint goals and tasks, ensuring their work integrates with other teams' efforts.

  • Nexus Daily Scrum: A daily meeting to discuss progress, integration challenges, and dependencies.

    Example: Teams share updates, address blockers, and synchronize their work.

  • Nexus Sprint Review: A combined review of the integrated increment with stakeholders.

    Example: Teams demonstrate the integrated product increment and gather feedback.

  • Nexus Sprint Retrospective: A joint retrospective to reflect on the past sprint and identify improvement opportunities.

    Example: Teams discuss what went well, what didn't, and how to improve integration and collaboration.

Managing Nexus Artifacts

  • Nexus Sprint Backlog: An aggregated backlog of all the work planned for the sprint, focusing on integration tasks.

    Example: Continuously update the backlog to reflect the current state of integration and dependencies.

  • Integrated Increment: The combined output of all Scrum teams at the end of each sprint, which must meet the Definition of Done.

    Example: Perform integration testing and quality assurance throughout the sprint.

  • Nexus Goal: A shared objective that guides the Scrum teams throughout the sprint.

    Example: Use the Nexus Goal to guide decision-making and prioritization.
